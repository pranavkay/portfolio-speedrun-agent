# Content Kit — What to Prepare Before Building Your Portfolio

Gather these items **before** you start. Having your content ready means your portfolio will be live in 30 minutes instead of sitting half-empty for weeks.

---

## Must-Have (we need these to start)

### 1. Portfolio Pieces (5-10 items)
Your best work. Quality over quantity.

- **Videographers**: YouTube or Vimeo links to your top projects
- **Photographers**: High-resolution images (at least 1200px wide) or Instagram links
- **Designers**: Behance or Dribbble project links, or high-res screenshots

For each piece, note:
- Project title (e.g. "Nike Brand Film", "Sarah & James Wedding")
- Category (e.g. "Brand Film", "Portrait", "Branding")
- Is it featured? (your top 3-5 pieces)

### 2. Profile Photo
A professional headshot or portrait. Minimum 900x1200px. This appears in the About section.

### 3. Bio (1-2 paragraphs)
Who you are, what you do, who you work with. Write like you're talking to a potential client, not a robot.

**Good:** "I'm a cinematographer based in LA, specializing in brand films and commercials. I've worked with Nike, Airbnb, and a dozen startups who needed their story told with intention."

**Too generic:** "I am a creative professional with a passion for visual storytelling and a keen eye for detail."

### 4. Contact Method
At least one of:
- Email address
- WhatsApp number (with country code, e.g. +1234567890)
- Booking link (Calendly, Cal.com, Google Calendar)

### 5. Services (3-5 items)
What you offer, with a one-line description:
- "Brand Films — Story-driven films that sell feeling before fact."
- "Portrait Photography — Headshots and editorial that capture personality."
- "UI/UX Design — Research-driven interfaces that look great and work better."

---

## Nice-to-Have (can add later via the admin panel)

### Client Testimonials (2-3)
For each: client name, their role/company, and a 1-3 sentence quote.

### Social Media Links
Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, Behance, Dribbble, Vimeo, TikTok, Twitter/X — whatever you actively use.

### Hero Background
A striking image or video for the top of your site. Should be landscape (16:9) and high-res.

### Tagline
One line that appears under your name. Examples:
- "Cinematographer — Commercials & Brand Films"
- "Portrait & Wedding Photographer — NYC"
- "Brand Designer & Illustrator"

### Logo
If you have one. Otherwise the site uses your name styled as text.

---

## Tips

- **Less is more.** 6 great portfolio pieces beats 20 mediocre ones.
- **Write your bio in first person.** "I" not "they" — it's your site.
- **Pick your best work, not your most recent.** Feature timeless quality, not recency.
- **Use real testimonials.** Even informal ones from happy clients are better than none.
